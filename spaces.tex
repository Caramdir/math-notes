\section{Algebraic Geometry}

Fix a commutative ring $k$ and let $\catCRing$ be the category of commutative rings over $k$.

The category $\catAffine$ of \emph{affine schemes} is the opposite category of $\catCRing$:
\[
    \catAffine = \oppcat{\catCRing}.
\]

There are several possible Grothendieck topologies on $\catAffine$: Zariski, étale, fppf, fpqc, \ldots
The category of \emph{$k$-spaces} $\catSpaces$ is the category of sheaves of sets in the given topology.
That is, $\catSpaces$ is the full subcategory of $\catFun(\oppcat\catAffine, \catSet)$ consisting of functors $F\colon \oppcat\catAffine → \catSet$ such that whenever $\{U_i → X\}$ is a cover, then
\[
    F(X) → \prod_i F(U_i) \rightrightarrows \prod_{i,j} F(U_i ×_X U_j)
\]
is an equalizer.
